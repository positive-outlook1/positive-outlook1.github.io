Research Interests

I am focused on designing state estimation and control algorithms for different rigid objects under different limitations and conditions to achieve specific control results. Firstly, I pay attention to engineering applications. In the field of aerospace, I design the navigation and control algorithms for rigid spacecraft under aerospace-related practical constraints, e.g., the spacecraft is lost in space without inertial information, the onboard gyroscopes fail to measure the motion angular velocity, there exists torque saturation with the actuators, and so on. When considering the UAVs, I take different types of measurements into account, including visual information and other unusual measurements, and design corresponding algorithms. With the relaxed constraints, many problems can be solved using methods based on determinstic framework, like the observer for SLAM. Secondly, I seek the properties of the results. Making use of the common control techniques, I am committed to obtaining algorithms with different levels of stabilityï¼Œsuch as global exponential stability and global finite-time stability. Therefore, I pay equal attention to engineering applications and theoretical innovations. Finally, I am also interested in the methods based on framework of stochastic system when dealing with state estimation, such as the family of Kalman filters.

Revolving these goals, my research interests focus on:

  • Spacecraft dynamics, navigation and control
  • State estimation and control for rigid body
  • Visual navigation
  • Theoretical techniques towards stronger stability
  • Kalman filters
